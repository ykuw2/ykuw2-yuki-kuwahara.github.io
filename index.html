About Me

Hey! My name is Yuki Kuwahara and I am currently a senior at UC Berkeley majoring in Data Science. I was born in Japan, but grew up in Southern California, specifically in the city of Irvine. I recieved my High School Diploma with the Seal of Biliteracy (in Japanese) from University High School. I am an avid tennis player, as I formerly played competitively in junior tournaments, and now as a member of California Club Tennis, one of the top university club tennis teams in the nation.

My Data Science journey started quite differently than others. I initially attended to University of California, Irvine, pursuing a major in Mathematics. However because of the pandemic, I started to learn Python on my own, which peaked my interest into the world of Data Science. I then took a class called Math 10 (Introduction to Python Programming for Data Science) there, which made me want to pursue studying Data Science solely, deciding me to transfer to UC Berkeley. I don't regret this decision rather, I am glad that I made this decision as I learned many knowledge and skills of Data Science that I would have never imagined of.

Recently, I've been interning at SwingVision, a start-up company bringing AI to tennis, in the San Francisco Bay Area. Currently in school, I am taking few classes with the notable ones being Ind Eng 142A (Introduction to Machine Learning and Data Analytics) and Info 159 (Natural Language Processing), both of which I am highly interested in and am excited to learn about.

And in the future, I hope to work in the tech world as a Data Analyst or Data Scientist!
